# S&W Admin Platform Demo Script

**Audience:** Internal team leads, operations managers, potential stakeholders
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes (plus Q&A)
**Format:** Live walkthrough on the production platform
**Presenter prep:** Log in as an admin-role user before the demo begins. Have the chat bubble closed and the Dashboard visible as the starting screen.

---

## Opening (1-2 minutes)

> This is the S&W Admin Console — a single workspace where scheduling, communication, hiring, contacts, and social media come together. Everything you are about to see is live, running against real data.

> The platform has three layers:
>
> 1. An **AI assistant** that sits on every page and can perform real work — not just answer questions.
> 2. A **crew scheduler** with a three-stage job workflow that takes a bid sheet all the way to daily crew packets.
> 3. A **social media manager** that drafts, schedules, and tracks content using your actual business context.

> I will walk through each one and show how they connect.

---

## Part 1 — AI Chat Assistant (5-6 minutes)

### 1A. Open the assistant

- Click the **blue chat bubble** in the bottom-right corner.
- Point out the panel header: "S&W Assistant — Jobs, leads, schedules, contacts, and content."

**Talking point:**
> The assistant is available from any admin page. It knows who you are, what role you have, and what data you are allowed to see. An operations lead will see different prompt cards than someone in HR or social media.

### 1B. Run a suggested prompt

- Click the **"Plan the schedule"** prompt card.
- Wait for the response — the assistant will pull tomorrow's schedule data and show a summary with rig counts, crew assignments, and gaps.

**Talking point:**
> This is not a generic chatbot. It reads directly from the crew scheduler database. Rig counts, crew gaps, and job assignments are live numbers — not templates.

### 1C. Show a write action

- Type: **"I need to enter a new job from a bid sheet."**
- The assistant returns a structured surface — an inline form for job intake.
- Fill in a sample job: name, job number, customer, contractor.
- Submit the surface.

**Talking point:**
> The assistant did not just describe what to do — it opened a working surface inside the chat. The job is now in the system. This is the first stage of a three-stage workflow: intake, detail, then scheduling.

### 1D. Show conversation history

- Click **"+ New"** in the panel header to start a fresh thread.
- Point out the **Previous threads** list that appears below the input area.
- Click a previous thread to show it loads the full history.

**Talking point:**
> Every conversation is stored and retrievable. Team members can pick up where they left off, and managers can review what work was done through the assistant.

### 1E. Close the chat panel

- Click the X to close. Transition to the full-page assistant later if time allows.

## Part 2 — Crew Scheduler (5-6 minutes)

### 2A. Navigate to Crew Scheduler

- Click **"Crew Scheduler"** in the top navigation bar.

### 2B. Schedule tab — daily rig planning

- The Schedule tab is the default view. Select **tomorrow's date**.
- Walk through one rig card:
  - Set rig status (Working).
  - Assign a job from the dropdown.
  - Assign a superintendent, truck, and crew members.
- Point out the overview bar: **Rigs Planned, Working Rigs, Crew Gaps, Available Crew**.

**Talking point:**
> Each rig is a card. You pick a status, assign a job, and build the crew. The system tracks who is already assigned so you can see crew gaps in real time. There is no double-booking — if a worker is on Rig 1, they show as unavailable for Rig 2.

### 2C. Copy a previous day

- Click the **"Copy Previous Day"** button.
- Show that yesterday's full schedule populates into today's cards.

**Talking point:**
> Most days start from the previous schedule with minor edits. This one button saves 20-30 minutes of manual re-entry every morning.

### 2D. Daily Packets tab

- Switch to the **"Daily Packets"** tab.
- Show the auto-generated per-crew-member packets: worker name, job details, contact info, cover sheet.
- Click **"Email All Packets"** (explain, but do not send during the demo).

**Talking point:**
> This is where the automation pays off. Once the schedule is saved, packets are generated automatically for every crew member. Each packet includes the job number, site address, superintendent contact, and a printable cover sheet. Emailing all of them is one click. This step alone used to take over an hour every afternoon — it now takes seconds.

### 2E. Live Planner tab (quick look)

- Switch to **"Live Planner"** to show the month-view calendar.
- Point out the activity heat map and the summary row: days with activity, rigs shown, crew slots filled.

**Talking point:**
> The planner gives you the birds-eye view. Filter by rig, worker, or job to spot patterns — who is overbooked, which rigs are idle, and where the gaps are.

## Part 3 — Social Media Manager (4-5 minutes)

### 3A. Navigate to Social Media

- Click **"Social Media"** in the top navigation bar.

### 3B. Brand Voice tab

- Click the **"Brand Voice"** tab (target icon).
- Show the tone profile: overall grade, messaging style, tone analysis.

**Talking point:**
> Before drafting anything, the platform understands your brand voice. This was generated from your existing content and can be updated any time. Every AI-drafted post runs through this voice profile so it sounds like your company — not a generic template.

### 3C. Posts tab — draft a post

- Switch to the **"Posts"** tab (pencil icon).
- Click to create a new post.
- Select post type: **"Project Showcase."**
- Type a one-line prompt: "Write a Facebook post about our latest foundation pour in Austin."
- Show the AI-generated draft with brand-consistent tone.

**Talking point:**
> You give it direction — it writes the first draft. The post type tells the AI whether this is a hiring post, a project showcase, or an industry tip, and it adjusts the tone and structure accordingly. You review, edit, and approve before anything goes live.

### 3D. Queue tab — scheduling

- Switch to the **"Queue"** tab (clipboard icon).
- Show posts in various states: pending, approved, scheduled.
- Point out the platform targeting (Facebook, LinkedIn).

**Talking point:**
> Approved posts move to the queue where you set the publish date and platform. The status flow is clear: pending, approved, scheduled, published. Nothing goes out without explicit approval.

### 3E. Chat tab — strategy conversation

- Switch to the **"Chat"** tab (speech bubble icon).
- Type: "What kind of posts should we focus on this month?"
- Show the AI response using real business context (recent projects, open positions, seasonal patterns).

**Talking point:**
> The social media chat is not just a copywriter. It has access to your job listings, recent projects, and content history. It can recommend a content calendar based on what is actually happening in the business right now.

## Wrap-up — How it connects (2 minutes)

> Let me show how these three pieces work together.
>
> 1. A new job comes in from a bid sheet. The **assistant** creates the job record through the chat.
> 2. As the job progresses, operational detail gets added in the **crew scheduler** — crew, rigs, trucks, contacts.
> 3. Once the job is underway, the **social media manager** can draft a project showcase post using the real job data — location, scope, customer — without anyone retyping it.
>
> This is one workflow, one database, one login. No spreadsheets passed between departments, no re-entering the same data in three different tools.

## Q&A Preparation

### Likely questions and suggested answers

**Q: Who can see what? Can a crew member see payroll or sales data?**
> Every user has a role — admin, operations, HR, social media, sales, or viewer. The platform enforces these at the data level. An HR user only sees the crew scheduler. A social media user sees the social media manager and image tools. A viewer sees the assistant chat only. Admins see everything.

**Q: What happens if the AI gives a wrong answer or makes a mistake?**
> The assistant cannot publish, send, or delete anything without you clicking a confirm button. Draft posts require approval before they enter the queue. Schedule changes require you to hit Save. The AI proposes — you approve.

**Q: Can we import our existing schedule spreadsheets?**
> Yes. The crew scheduler supports bulk job import from Excel or CSV. You paste the data, map the columns, and it creates the records. The same works for crew member lists.

**Q: Is the chat history saved? Can my manager see my conversations?**
> Conversations are stored per user and session. You can view your own previous threads and switch between them. Currently, only admin-role users could query another user's history through the database — there is no cross-user chat viewer in the UI.

**Q: Does the social media manager actually post to Facebook?**
> It connects to Facebook through an OAuth integration. Posts move through an approval queue before publishing. LinkedIn support is in the targeting options as well.

**Q: How does the packet automation work for new employees?**
> Add the crew member in the Manage panel (name, phone, role), then assign them to a rig in the Schedule tab. Their packet generates automatically the next time the schedule is saved.

**Q: What if we need to change the schedule after packets were sent?**
> Update the schedule and re-send packets. The system generates from the current state — there is no lock after sending. The new packets replace the old ones in the recipients' inboxes.

**Q: Can I use this on my phone in the field?**
> The admin console is responsive. The schedule view, chat bubble, and packet viewer all work on mobile browsers. There is no separate app to install.

**Q: What AI model powers the assistant?**
> The assistant uses Groq for fast inference. It has access to your live database — crew jobs, contacts, submissions, career listings, schedule data — and can read and write through a set of controlled tools. It cannot run arbitrary queries or access data outside its defined scope.

**Q: How do we get started / what does onboarding look like?**
> The platform is already running against your data. Onboarding is: (1) set up your user account with the right role, (2) walk through the three-stage job workflow once with a real job, and (3) use the assistant for a week to build familiarity. I will be available for questions throughout.
